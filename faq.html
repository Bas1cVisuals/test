FAQ.

Operational parameters, standard protocols, and execution metrics for structural editing workflows.

01 // VIDEO PARAMETERS

What type of videos do you edit?
I execute formatting for Reels/Shorts, YouTube long-form content, and structural promo/corporate edits.

02 // VELOCITY

What is the turnaround time?
Reels/Shorts: 24–48 hours standard. YouTube long-form: 2–5 days, contingent upon sequence length and raw data volume.

03 // REVISIONS

What is the revision policy?
Each project lifecycle includes up to 2 revisions. Additional structural modifications require supplementary billing.

04 // REQUIRED DATA

What input is required from the client?
Raw footage link (Drive/Dropbox), reference visual style, brand assets (logos/typography), and specific editorial directives/cut notes.

05 // TRANSMISSION

How should footage be routed?
Primary routing: Google Drive, Dropbox, or WeTransfer. WhatsApp routing is strictly limited to low-resolution proxy transfers.

06 // TYPOGRAPHY

Do you integrate captions/subtitles?
Yes. I implement clean, high-contrast, kinetic typography (modern, bold, highlighted vectors) for short-form retention.

07 // STATIC ASSETS

Is thumbnail generation available?
Yes. Static visual packaging and thumbnail generation can be executed as a supplementary package.

08 // RENDER SPECS

What is the final export format?
Reels/Shorts: 1080×1920 (9:16). YouTube: 1920×1080 (16:9). Rendered and delivered as high-bitrate MP4.

09 // AUDIO LICENSING

How is music and copyright handled?
I utilize copyright-safe audio banks. If the client provisions an external copyrighted track, the client assumes full publishing liability.

10 // BILLING METRICS

How is pricing calculated?
Cost scales based on: final timeline length, raw footage volume, motion graphic complexity, and deadline proximity.

11 // PAYMENT STRUCTURE

What are the payment terms?
Standard: 50% initiation deposit + 50% upon delivery of the primary cut. Small tasks require 100% upfront clearance.

12 // RETAINERS

Is monthly editing available?
Yes. Continuous operational packages (weekly/monthly) are available for clients maintaining regular content pipelines.
